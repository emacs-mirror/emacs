ABSTRACT:

Conventional wisdom, that smaller componets contain relatively fewer faults, 
may be wrong.  This author found that medium-sized components were 
proportionately more reliable that small or large ones.  Moreover, he says, 
there may be limits on the fault density we can acheive.
